The problem

Help us figure out if our idea of a Fitbit for horses is worth pursuing.

A showjump scientist had the idea of creating Fitbit for horses, she had an investor but he had doubts about investing, so I was approached to help answer some important business questions, after an interview with both of them I decided a design sprint was a good solution to their problem. This is because it is an activity that they will both participate in, use the opportunity to learn more about the project and also figure out how to work together on the project, The result will also answer both their questions, giving them a clear path for moving forward on the project.

Planning & Strategy

The strategy for this project is simple, I planned a one-week sprint session with the showjump scientist and her investor, she was the decider, and I was the facilitator. We ran the sprint out in the countryside near the showjump scientists barn to enable us to have access to horses. We also used the show jump scientists Facebook group for our research

Pre sprint

I sent out a form to the stakeholder to help find users we can use to test the end product, and I gathered all research resources, conducted the necessary customer interviews, before the sprint, purchased all materials needed for the sprint, and prepared guiding process to help the stakeholders through the sprint.
